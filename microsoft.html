Microsoft Corporation, founded by Bill Gates and Paul Allen in 1975, is a multinational technology company based in Redmond, Washington. Originally a software vendor, it became famous for its Windows operating system and Office productivity suite. Over the years, Microsoft has diversified into hardware, cloud computing, and AI. The company operates as a publicly traded entity, with shares listed on the NASDAQ under the symbol MSFT. Its average compensation package varies widely by role and location but typically includes a base salary, bonuses, and stock options. Microsoft is also known for its strategic acquisitions. The purchase of LinkedIn in 2016 expanded its presence in the professional networking space, while the acquisition of GitHub in 2018 bolstered its role in the developer community. More recently, the acquisition of Activision Blizzard in 2022 underscored its commitment to expanding its footprint in the gaming industry.

1. Online Application


    Submission: Submit your resume through Microsoft’s career portal or a campus recruitment drive.
    Screening: The resume is screened for relevant skills, experience, and educational background.

2. Online Assessment

  • Data Structure Coding Challenges: Typically involves 5 to 6 competitive coding questions(DSA)
  • Platforms: May be hosted on platforms like HackerRank, CodeSignal, or Microsoft’s own platform.

3. Technical Phone/Video Interview

  • Technical Interview Coding Questions: Expect questions similar to those in the online assessment.
  • Technical Discussion: Discussion around algorithms, data structures, and problem-solving strategies.
  • Behavioral Questions: Some questions may assess your experiences, projects and team skills.

4. Onsite Interviews

    • Technical Coding Interviews: Similar to the phone interview but with more complex problems.
    • System Design: About designing systems,understanding architecture,and making design decisions.
    • Problem-Solving: Advanced problem-solving questions that test your understanding of DSA in depth.
  • Behavioral Interviews: Focus on cultural fit,leadership,and how you handle real-world scenarios.
    • Projects: Detailed discussion of your past projects, including your role, challenges faced.
    • Situational Questions: Questions on conflict resolution, team dynamics, and leadership experiences.

5. Technical-cum HR Round

  • Involves Technical Questions and HR Questions: This round may combine technical & HR Round.
    • About your Career Goals: Where you see yourself in the future, your long-term career objectives.
    • Strength & Weakness: Self-assessment questions that explore your personal growth and improvement.
    • Company Fit: Why you want to work at Microsoft and how your values align with the company’s mission and culture.

Preparation Tips

  • Practice Coding and DSA: Solve problems on platforms like LeetCode, HackerRank, or CodeChef.
  • Study System Design: Read up on system design principles and practice designing scalable systems.
  • Review Behavioral Questions: Prepare answers for common behavioral interview questions and reflect on past experiences.
  • Research Microsoft: Understand Microsoft’s products, culture, and recent developments to discuss intelligently why you want to work there.
